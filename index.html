Learning from Making Mistakes

Portfolio Entry # 1

The scientific method is one lesson that doesn't let go even until college. I'm not complaining about it is a systematic way of finding the truth and cannot be forgotten. However, the scientific method today is turning more into a guideline rather than a process through which all scientific studies must undergo. Coming from a science high school, I had a first­hand experience as to how rigorous the process is. When writing a scientific research paper, you has to triple­check your data, make sure you're processing them right, and you can't always place right hypotheses. It's worth thinking about how scientists work in the real world. It isn't a surprise that some of them resort to falsifying or cherry­picking the results of their data. There are deadlines to meet and quotas to fill so that they can sustain their research with the money earned from publishing papers, not to mention their pride when they get a good finding. The scientific method exists to stop these botched studies by allowing other scientists to confirm new findings and not allow false ones to propagate misinformation and cause harm.

The cholesterol study that was discussed in class interested me so I looked more into it. It turns out cholesterol is essential in keeping us alive and is used in the production of some of our body's hormones. The scientist that started the idea that cholesterol is a factor in heart disease is Ancel Keys. He argued that cholesterol and saturated fat consumption is related to heart disease with his “Seven Countries” test. Given that Keys was the creator of the ration that nourished U.S. soldiers in the second world war, his controversial study was supported by a handful of US senators despite the questionable validity of the study. Keys's study which should not have made far in the scientific community has become the basis for the standard low­fat, cholesterol­free, “healthy” American diet. Despite the measures taken to decrease cholesterol, obesity and heart disease are still part of the biggest problems that United States of America faces today. It turns out that when the cholesterol was limited by the U.S. government, food manufacturers had replaced cholesterol with sugar and processed carbs, the true causes of heart disease (along with a sedentary lifestyle). Instead of Keys saving his fellow Americans from a future of bad diets, he has done the opposite.

The scientific method isn't something we should take for granted. It's the reason the human race has advanced so far. Although it is organized, there is still some margin for error and we can't be sure if a new finding is completely true. However, with the method there are no mistakes in science which can't be turned into useful accidents that can further the inquiry into our natural world.
