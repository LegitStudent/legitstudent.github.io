Encryption in 21 st Century Telecommunications | Portfolio Entry # 1

The Internet is currently one of the most popular ways for people to communicate with each other. It is faster than writing snail mail, and more accessible throughout the world than using phone networks. Services like Facebook and Viber provide a free and friendly platform for people to take advantage of the Internet’s capabilities. The Internet will remain with us for a long time which should make us think of its implications on our society. Privacy is an issue that continually becomes more relevant due to the open nature of the Internet. Anything posted online can go “viral”, or be spread out to a large audience whether the original poster intended it or not. Furthermore, seemingly private sessions, like two-person chatting or email, aren’t as exclusive as they really are. We have to have privacy online not because we engage in illegal activities, but because it is a human right. Thus, we need to be able to secure whatever we do on the Internet, and encryption fulfills this need.

Encryption works by getting a message, and changing it into a form such that only the sender and the recipient could understand it. A basic form of encryption would be the use of a cipher. An example of a cipher is a Caesar cipher which takes each letter in a message shifts it three times to right of the letter’s position in the alphabet. “Hello world” would become “Ebiil tloia”. The recipient would have to be aware of the cipher in order to decode the message. Modern day encryption algorithms are more complicated and harder to solve, but function with the same principle.

Nutrition and Me | Portfolio Entry # 2

Nutrition is important that it actually shapes our lives as human beings. The scope of the study of nutrition is very broad and can get very technical and complicated, but it suggests the very simple and understandable concept of keeping a balance between how much energy from the food we take in and how much of those we use. Since we can’t possibly avoid eating for we need to do it to live, the study of nutrition will surely remain with us for as long as the human race exists.

I have had my own share of experience in nutrition. It was last summer when I stumbled upon an article that talks about how we tend to neglect physical activity nowadays and rarely watch what we eat. This motivated me to research more into the topic, which lead me into knowing more about nutrition. Firstly, I learned about the three macronutrients, protein, fat, and carbohydrates. Then, there were formulas for calculating the total daily energy expenditure of a person, and another one for the distribution of calories among the macronutrients. It was really satisfying because it was so concrete and better than the vague advice given by a lot of people to “just eat less and exercise”.

The actual challenge to proper nutrition is usually not the numbers or the knowledge because the resources that teach actual nutrition are actually really easy to understand. For me, the obstacle lay in my mind. I got my plan, and on paper the path was pretty much laid out for me but there was still something that was stopping me from actually going for it. The plan included a drastic change in diet that even though I know it’s healthy, is hard to accept. From here we can see how our own image becomes really linked with what we eat. We tend to resist changes in what we get used to, like how we go to school and the manner of our speaking. Big changes never really yield productive results so, the best course of action anyone that tries to get their nutrition right is to follow it little by little.

Even though we could simplify the lessons of proper nutrition down to eating the right amount of nutrients and getting a good amount of physical activity, it does not become that simple when we begin to put it into practice. Developing a proper lifestyle through a good diet was never really a goal you can attain by just simply lying around all day and waiting for a miracle to happen. Anything worthwhile is never going to be easy, and surely proper nutrition would lead to a healthy body. As my mother would tell me over and over again, we only have one body and one life so we need to make it count.

Intellectual Exploring and Exploiting Biotechnology | Porfolio Entry # 3

Where will we be without biotechnology? The fields of science that made a big difference in our society are often taken for granted because they have done a great job of integrating themselves in our current lives. One of these fields is biotechnology which has been with us since the dawn of ancient civilizations. Innovations in this field span from the early methods of fermentation for wine, to modern day experiments with the genetics of living organisms.

Biotechnology is both wonderful and humbling. We still have a lot to find out about the world with all its surprises and mysteries. An estimated majority of species here on Earth has not been discovered yet which brings up the possibility of developing new enhancements to our already revolutionary lifestyles.

We must remember that humanity was never always put in a position that favors it. The world can be harsh, but a little creative biotechnology from us solves this problem and ensures the survival of our species.

To Know and To Do: Lifestyle Choices | Portfolio Entry # 4

Although a bit radical, the effect of science on our lifestyles can be shown by the power of the atom. Through years of scientific research, we have seen how much power an atom could release. In this situation, you could say knowledge is power. Depending on whoever holds it, he could develop an efficient method of electricity generation or a destructive weapon.

Our recent lectures in lifestyle show that while science could be the cause of some of the human lifestyle problems, such as the production of alcohol by fermentation, it could also form remedies for some of us through developments in sexually-transmitted disease research and helpful medical warnings on smoking.

Lifestyle choices really fall on us. As discussed in the first lectures, scientific research does not tell us how to use the knowledge. Knowing the fact that smoking tobacco can lead to lung cancer does not stop the millions of smokers worldwide. We can just say that we are fortunate that this information is available to us, and maybe this could cause some of us to reconsider some of what we do.
