Encryption in 21 st Century Telecommunications | Portfolio Entry # 1

The Internet is currently one of the most popular ways for people to communicate with each other. It is faster than writing snail mail, and more accessible throughout the world than using phone networks. Services like Facebook and Viber provide a free and friendly platform for people to take advantage of the Internet’s capabilities. The Internet will remain with us for a long time which should make us think of its implications on our society. Privacy is an issue that continually becomes more relevant due to the open nature of the Internet. Anything posted online can go “viral”, or be spread out to a large audience whether the original poster intended it or not. Furthermore, seemingly private sessions, like two-person chatting or email, aren’t as exclusive as they really are. We have to have privacy online not because we engage in illegal activities, but because it is a human right. Thus, we need to be able to secure whatever we do on the Internet, and encryption fulfills this need.

Encryption works by getting a message, and changing it into a form such that only the sender and the recipient could understand it. A basic form of encryption would be the use of a cipher. An example of a cipher is a Caesar cipher which takes each letter in a message shifts it three times to right of the letter’s position in the alphabet. “Hello world” would become “Ebiil tloia”. The recipient would have to be aware of the cipher in order to decode the message. Modern day encryption algorithms are more complicated and harder to solve, but function with the same principle.

Nutrition and Me | Portfolio Entry # 2

Nutrition is important that it actually shapes our lives as human beings. The scope of the study of nutrition is very broad and can get very technical and complicated, but it suggests the very simple and understandable concept of keeping a balance between how much energy from the food we take in and how much of those we use. Since we can’t possibly avoid eating for we need to do it to live, the study of nutrition will surely remain with us for as long as the human race exists.

As much as the problems about our environment is actually really big on the outside, it is just as big in the inside. One question we could ask is if we are truly worried about the earth? Is “Mother Nature” actually in danger? The basis of our anxiety for environmental problems may be rooted in our instinct for survival. We may have gone through periods of rational thought and enlightenment, but our evolved brain is still hardwired for survival. It may be that we are not aiming to save the world, but rather we aim to save to world to save ourselves.

In the long run, humanity might not be that significant. The geological time scale could span billions of years, whereas our survival cannot afford that security as of this moment. Environmental problems threaten this surviva, which is where the topic of sustainable development comes in.

Sustainable development is simplified as “leaving enough for the next one”. We develop a kind of selflessness even if it's only among our own kind.
